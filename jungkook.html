"They say the hexagon is the most perfect shape. But there’s a crack in mine.
It’s not perfect. It wants to be, but it keeps breaking."



If you drew a self-portrait, how would you draw yourself?

Mmm…a hexagon that’s broken, cracked.



I feel as though I know what you mean, but could you describe it in your own words?

I’m always wanting to be perfect and I’m always trying to climb higher. I do think I have enough talent, but I also believe I’m lazy. I have two completely contradictory tendencies. I want to climb higher, but at the same time, I don’t want to. They say the hexagon is the most perfect shape. But there’s a crack in mine. It’s not perfect. It wants to be, but it keeps breaking.



A hexagon with no cracks?

That’s right. Maybe the pinnacle is when I’m finally content with myself. Say I was content with everything right now. Then I’d have nothing to wish for. I wouldn’t want to climb higher either. Some day when a moment like that comes, maybe it would mean I’ve finally reached the top?

Thoughts

Jungkook's answers resonates with me because I too have perfectionist tendencies. But I'm also a procrastinator at heart, which is a perfect match, haha. But in all seriousness, as the oldest child in my family, I'm expected to be perfect in every way, to not let anyone down. But at the same time, sometimes I don't want to be.

It's a whole cycle of wanting to be perfect and trying and when it doesn't work out, just getting discouraged and not trying the next time. What Jungkook says - "Maybe the pinnacle is when I'm finally content with myself" - is ideally the goal. We live with ourselves, and humans aren't perfect. Expecting to be perfect doesn't do anything for anyone. Not meeting the expectations for ourselves just makes us feel worse about ourselves. Ultimately what we should do is give ourselves grace and be content with how we are.
